Atlas of Living Australia Style Guide

Typography

Headings

  • use Roboto, with a font weight of 200
  • use sentence case for headings
  • use headings consistently to create a clear hierarchy

Lead paragraph

There should only be one lead paragraph per page.

A 24px lead paragraph. Maecenas sed diam eget risus varius blandit sit amet non magna. Donec ullamcorper nulla non metus auctor fringilla.

A 19px body copy paragraph. Maecenas sed diam eget risus varius blandit sit amet non magna. Donec ullamcorper nulla non metus auctor fringilla. Maecenas sed diam eget risus varius blandit sit amet non magna. Donec ullamcorper nulla non metus auctor fringilla.

syntax: class="lead"

Body copy

  • use Roboto, with a font weight of 200
  • avoid using bold and italics
  • use 19px for body copy – 16px for smaller screens
  • use smaller sizes only if there's a user need (eg 16px, 14px, 12px)
  • make sure lines of text don't exceed 75 characters, as they become harder to read beyond that point
  • for example, on this page – text blocks are two-thirds of the page width

A 19px body copy paragraph. Maecenas sed diam eget risus varius blandit sit amet non magna. Donec ullamcorper nulla non metus auctor fringilla.

A 16px supporting text paragraph. Maecenas sed diam eget risus varius blandit sit amet non magna. Donec ullamcorper nulla non metus auctor fringilla.

syntax: use class="word-limit" to limit the length of a paragraph. This can be applied to any div element.

Links

  • links within body copy should be red (#C44D34;) and underlined on hover
  • links without surrounding text should not have a full stop at the end
  • link colours can be found in the colour palette

A 19px link without surrounding text.

A 19px body copy link. Fusce dapibus, tellus ac cursus commodo, tortor mauris condimentum nibh, ut fermentum massa justo sit amet risus.

A 19px body copy external link. Fusce dapibus, tellus ac cursus commodo, tortor mauris condimentum nibh, ut fermentum massa justo sit amet risus.

List items start with a lowercase letter and have no full stop at the end.

Inset text

Use bordered inset text to draw attention to important content on the page.

What is the Atlas of Living Australia (Atlas)?

The Atlas is an information system that aggregates data on all Australian species of flora and fauna and provides a capability to analyse the data geospatially.

The above heading and paragraph combination uses an h1 with a .heading-xlarge class and the paragraph uses the .lead class.

Why should I register?

Without permission from the owner, others cannot use something that is covered by copyright. The license documents your ownership, the conditions you place on others using it and how you would like to be acknowledged as the owner.

Registering with the Atlas allows you to:

  • provide feedback on the Atlas and its content
  • record an observation of a species, upload a photograph or share information on a species
  • save an analysis in the spatial portal so you can return to it later
  • subscribe to updates about the Atlas.

The above heading, paragraph and unordered list combination uses an h2 with a class .heading-large and standard p and ul tag elements.

What is licensing?

Put simply, licensing is granting certain rights to others to allow them to use your work or data. You do not give up ownership of your information by doing this (unless you specifically release your work into the public domain); you just allow others to use it for their own research and analysis.

In relation to the Atlas, you may need to grant the Atlas and its users, a license to use you data.

Anything that is considered a ‘work’ is covered by copyright and so the Atlas needs permission to be able to display and provide access to it.
